Format per card: **Competence** · similes (front) · situations (back, shortened versions from the sheet).

## 1. Spotting Opportunities
- Similes: "Like finding a diamond in the rough" · "Like discovering a hidden shortcut" · "Like finding money in an old coat pocket"
- Situations: A founder finds an untapped niche through networking and casual conversation. · A start-up grows faster by using an existing platform instead of building one. · Spotting shifts in consumer interest, like rising demand for eco-friendly goods. · A founder uses unused assets, like empty office space, to drive growth. · A new entrepreneur launches a product early by spotting a social media trend. · An entrepreneur notices an underserved niche in local customer habits.

## 2. Creativity
- Similes: "Like making something out of nothing" · "Like mixing ingredients to create a new dish" · "Like building a castle in the sand"
- Situations: Developing an app that users can customize to fit their needs and preferences. · Combining influencers and crowdfunding for a unique product launch. · Innovating on a budget by prototyping with low-cost materials and iterating. · Pitching investors during a downturn while adapting strategies in real-time. · Repurposing AI from facial recognition to optimize warehouse logistics. · A small business owner scales by automating workflows with no-code tools.

## 3. Vision
- Similes: "Like plotting stars to navigate uncharted seas" · "Like imagining a city in an empty field" · "Like imagining a seed grow into a forest"
- Situations: Planning a 5-year expansion for a start-up in a new industry with no benchmarks. · Spotting a disruptive opportunity where others see a crowded market. · Shaping company culture early, knowing it drives long-term success. · Taking on high-risk innovation, trusting learning to guide success. · Seeing social issues online and envisioning a business to solve them. · Reimagining your path to impact by learning from a competitor's success.

## 4. Valuing Ideas
- Similes: "Like examining a treasure map for hidden value" · "Like spotting gold in a pile of rocks" · "Like selecting the key pieces in a jigsaw puzzle"
- Situations: Seeing value in a patent or intellectual property that others miss. · Finding a solid business model in a struggling start-up. · Improving an idea through customer feedback and strategic pivots. · Seeing how a partnership strengthens the offer and drives growth. · Identifying an overlooked asset or trend and rethinking its potential. · Recognizing a simple, effective solution and scaling its impact.

## 5. Ethical & Sustainable Thinking
- Similes: "Like planting a tree for the next generation" · "Like walking randomly through a poor neighbourhood" · "Like choosing a path through a fragile forest"
- Situations: Launching a business focused on sustainability, like biodegradable packaging. · Scaling responsibly while upholding values and reducing environmental harm. · Forming ethical partnerships without risking brand integrity. · Handling IP theft professionally while strengthening security. · Making ethical choices despite financial struggles, like pricier eco-options. · Expanding while neglecting environmental rules can harm reputation.

## 6. Self-Awareness & Self-Efficacy
- Similes: "Like looking into a mirror and seeing your potential" · "Like testing yourself in a safe space" · "Like climbing a mountain to discover yourself"
- Situations: Using storytelling skills to inspire and lead a team. · Practicing pitches with mentors before facing investors. · Reflecting on resilience after setbacks in the start-up phase. · Feeling calm and clear after resolving a major team conflict. · A solopreneur builds confidence in speaking and successfully pitches to investors. · An entrepreneur simplifies complex topics and launches an educational platform.

## 7. Motivation & Perseverance
- Similes: "Like running a marathon in a storm" · "Like pushing a broken-down car uphill" · "Like trying to hold water in your hands"
- Situations: Staying focused on the start-up's long-term vision despite market fluctuations. · Maintaining strong ties with customers and investors, even in slow growth. · Staying optimistic and adjusting the business model after a product failure. · Learning from a lost client to improve the value proposition. · Adapting to customer churn by refining the offer to meet evolving needs. · Finding alternative funding after missing a key deadline.

## 8. Mobilizing Resources
- Similes: "Like gathering wood to build a fire" · "Like using only the ingredients you have to cook a meal for everyone" · "Like finding puzzle pieces to complete the picture"
- Situations: Getting friends and family to invest early in the start-up. · Using crowdfunding, grants, and other resources to fund growth. · Partnering with complementary start-ups to offer a full solution. · Handling cash flow shortages by securing quick bridge funding. · A founder bootstraps by using freelancers, renting gear, and bartering. · Sharing unused resources in a network to reach a common goal.

## 9. Financial & Economic Literacy
- Similes: "Like packing a suitcase with strict weight limits" · "Like planning a road trip on a tight budget" · "Like stretching a meal to last the whole week"
- Situations: Balancing growth and cashflow during early scaling. · A founder bootstraps by efficiently allocating limited resources. · Forecasting granular expenses like office supplies or shipping. · Feeling the joy of securing an investment at a key growth moment. · A freelancer tracks software costs and keeps only valuable tools. · A small business owner adjusts pricing after learning true costs.

## 10. Mobilizing Others
- Similes: "Like lighting a spark to ignite a fire" · "Like inviting friends to a celebration" · "Like a magnet drawing others together"
- Situations: Inspiring a team to support a high-risk product launch. · Aligning the team's workflow with partners to ensure smooth execution. · Building a strong team through a purpose-driven culture. · Managing disappointment when a key partner backs out last minute. · Celebrating a milestone after a tough campaign earns surprise recognition. · A founder structures a team to maximize expertise and efficiency.

## 11. Taking the Initiative
- Similes: "Like stepping into the breach — a tough situation" · "Like diving into a pool before testing the water" · "Like being the first onto the dance floor"
- Situations: Volunteering to present at an event instead of waiting for an invite. · Releasing an MVP to quickly test the market. · Stepping into operations to fix logistics before the team acts. · Securing a key partnership before a competitor moves in. · Diving into a project with little knowledge, learning hands-on. · Taking the lead on an effort others hesitate to tackle, despite risks.

## 12. Planning & Management
- Similes: "Like setting up dominoes so they all fall perfectly in sequence" · "Like choreographing a dance where every step must sync perfectly with the music" · "Like a wedding planner sets the table"
- Situations: Planning a startup's roadmap while balancing limited resources. · Creating a clear pitch deck to attract investors. · Hosting a structured product launch event. · Balancing investor and team expectations during scaling talks. · Aligning team efforts across priorities in peak growth. · Adjusting after underestimating time for a regulatory deadline.

## 13. Coping with Uncertainty, Ambiguity & Risk
- Similes: "Like walking through fog without a flashlight" · "Like playing a game of cards blindfolded" · "Like sketching the first lines of a masterpiece"
- Situations: Expanding into a new market with little data on customer preferences. · Choosing product features without solid customer feedback. · Entering a new market with little prior experience. · Unforeseeable regulatory delays slow progress. · Facing investor absence after months of preparation. · Balancing market shifts in demands while stabilizing operations.

## 14. Working with Others
- Similes: "Like a rowing team pulling in unison to win the race" · "Like a kitchen staff during a dinner rush — everyone plays a role, or the service breaks down" · "Like a beehive where every bee has a job, but the hive only thrives when they work together"
- Situations: Working with co-founders to align on a shared vision for investors. · Coordinating departments seamlessly during a key product launch. · Uniting diverse team perspectives to tackle complex challenges. · Strengthening team morale by celebrating small wins together. · Communicating well and aligned processes keep remote teams motivated across time zones. · A startup's leadership ensures marketing, product, and sales stay in sync.

## 15. Learning through Experience
- Similes: "Like falling and learning to stand again" · "Like stumbling mid-race, then finding your stride" · "Like baking without a recipe"
- Situations: Adjusting the business model after failure and moving forward. · Enhancing prototypes based on customer feedback. · Optimizing marketing after reviewing past results. · Refining strategy after analyzing what went wrong. · Solving problems on the spot, learning hands-on. · Enhancing products or services based on early feedback.
